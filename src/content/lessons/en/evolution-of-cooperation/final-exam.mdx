This is the whole course in one sitting. Every idea you've met — the cold paradox that costly helping should be bred out of existence, the shadow of the future that quietly rewrites the payoffs so cooperation becomes the selfish move, Axelrod's four-line winner that never won a single duel yet won the war, the noise that traps two nice strategies in an endless feud, the five roads beyond simple reciprocity, and the honest catalogue of where the model lies — shows up here as questions. Hold the through-line in your head: cooperation among selfish agents is never magic, it is what selection builds the moment the game stops being one-shot and anonymous — when a future, a memory, a reputation, or a bloodline changes the payoffs. <Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
You now hold the whole model. The **paradox** is real: blind selection rewards whatever out-breeds, so **costly helping** ($c > 0$) looks like suicide, and a naive **Always Cooperate** world is invaded and erased by one **Always Defect** mutant — it is **not an ESS**. The rescue is the **shadow of the future**: repeat the dilemma ($T > R > P > S$, i.e. 5, 3, 1, 0) and a defection today forfeits all of tomorrow's cooperation, so when the **continuation probability w** clears its threshold, **cooperating becomes the selfish move** — though a *known, finite* endpoint unravels it through **backward induction** (real, open-ended horizons escape). Axelrod **ran a real tournament**, and the simplest four-line entry, **Tit-for-Tat**, won by being **nice, retaliatory, forgiving, and clear** — it never won a single duel yet **won the war by never losing badly**, while nasty strategies **poison their own well** and go extinct once their prey runs out. Add **noise** and one garbled move traps two Tit-for-Tats in an endless feud (unforgiving **Grim Trigger** suffers most), which the refinements — **generous** and **contrite** Tit-for-Tat and **Win-Stay-Lose-Shift (Pavlov)** — are built to survive by hitting the **forgiveness sweet spot**. Beyond partners who meet again lie **five roads**: **indirect reciprocity** (reputation and gossip), **kin selection** (**Hamilton's rule rB > C**, siblings $r = 1/2$, cousins $r = 1/8$), **network reciprocity** (cooperators survive by **clustering**), and the contested **multilevel group selection** — never the naive "for the good of the species." To **grow cooperation** you lengthen the shadow, make actions visible, let cooperators assort, and change the payoffs — **not moralise**. And stay honest about **where the model lies**: cooperation is **fragile and invadable**, the **one-shot-versus-repeated** confusion flips predictions, Tit-for-Tat is **noise-fragile**, and the **naturalistic fallacy** — mistaking *what evolved* for *what is good* — is a trap. Cooperation among selfish agents is not magic and not morality; it is what self-interest evolves into once the payoffs change.
