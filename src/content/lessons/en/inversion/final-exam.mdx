<Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
Inversion means asking how you’d *guarantee failure* and refusing to do those
things — a flip that works because failure modes come out specific and few while
“how to succeed” stays vague. Scale it up into a **pre-mortem** to defuse a project
before it starts, and into Munger’s strategy of **avoiding stupidity** to win the
loser’s games that make up most of life. Point it at yourself with **anti-goals**
and personal pre-mortems, but always run the **round trip** — aim forward, invert
to stress-test, safeguard, then advance — because inversion is *subtractive*: it
finds what to avoid, never the winning move. Know its limits: it biases toward
caution (wrong for bold asymmetric bets) and, overused, breeds paralysis. Invert,
fix, then move. *Invert — always invert — but never only invert.*
</Callout>
