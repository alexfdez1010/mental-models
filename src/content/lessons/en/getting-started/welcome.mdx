This template turns a subject into an **interactive, bilingual, zero-to-expert
course catalog**. Before you replace this example with your own subject, here's
the mental model in five minutes.

## Topics and lessons

A **topic** is a course about one subject (this page's topic is *Getting
Started*). A topic owns many **lessons**, ordered by their `order` frontmatter
field. URLs are slug-based and SEO-friendly:

- `/getting-started` → the topic landing page
- `/getting-started/welcome` → this lesson
- `/es/getting-started/welcome` → its Spanish twin

Every lesson is an MDX file under `src/content/lessons/<lang>/<topic>/`, with a
matching twin under `es/`. The English source and the Spanish twin always stay
in parity — same slug, same order, same components.

<Callout variant="info" title="Where things live">
Topics: `src/content/topics/<lang>/`. Lessons:
`src/content/lessons/<lang>/<topic>/`. The build queue of planned-but-unbuilt
courses: `src/lib/upcoming.ts`. The subject's mission and scope: `TOPIC.md`.
</Callout>

## The zero-to-expert ladder

Courses aren't islands — they form a path. Each topic declares a `difficulty`
(`beginner` → `intermediate` → `advanced` → `expert`) and a list of
`dependencies` (the slugs of prerequisite courses). The catalog draws those
dependencies as a roadmap-style graph, so the learning order is obvious at a
glance.

## How a new course gets built

You can author by hand (see the `new-lesson` skill) or let the autonomous cron
builder do it. Either way the flow is the same: a planned course lives as a data
entry in `src/lib/upcoming.ts` (it shows up as a dimmed "Coming soon" node on
the catalog). When it's built, its entry is deleted and the real topic MDX takes
its place.
