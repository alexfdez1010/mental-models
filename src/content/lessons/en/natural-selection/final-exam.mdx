This is the final exam for **Natural Selection**. It pulls the whole course together: the three-ingredient recipe (*variation, selection, heredity — repeat*) and why each ingredient is load-bearing, the compounding ratchet that turns a small survival edge into large directed change, what biologists *really* mean by **fitness** (the single most misread word in evolution), how fitness is on loan from the environment, what a **selection pressure** is and how reversing it reverses the march, the way the same blind algorithm runs far beyond biology — in markets, A/B tests, breeding, memes, code and your own immune system — and finally the three misreadings that fool nearly everyone: that evolution is goal-directed, that it works "for the good of the species," and that it's a ladder of progress climbing toward us. Take your time and reason each one through; several look easy until you spot the trap — an organism "trying" to adapt, "fittest" meaning "strongest," or "everyone repeats it" meaning "it's true."

<Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
Natural selection is an **algorithm**, not just a fact about animals: wherever
**variation**, **selection**, and **heredity** are all present, a population accumulates
whatever fits its environment — building design with no designer. **Variation** is the
*blind raw material* (it doesn’t arrive because it’s needed), **selection** is the
*direction*, and **heredity** is the *memory* that lets a small, consistent survival edge
compound into large change; knock out any one and the machine stalls. **Fitness** is the
most misread word in the model: it means *surviving offspring in a particular environment*,
never strength or "best" — and it’s on loan from the environment, so a **selection
pressure** can reverse and the very same trait flips from winner to doomed (the peppered
moth, pale → dark → pale). Because the algorithm is **substrate-neutral**, the same
three-question probe reads markets, A/B tests, breeding, memes (selected for spread, not
truth), code, and your own immune system. And the model’s eerie competence plants three
illusions you must refuse: it is **not teleological** (no foresight or goal), **not "for the
good of the species"** (it acts on individuals and genes leaving more offspring *now*), and
**not a ladder of progress** climbing toward humans (life is a branching bush, and "more
evolved" is meaningless) — and remember that describing how selection works never tells you
how things *ought* to be.
</Callout>
