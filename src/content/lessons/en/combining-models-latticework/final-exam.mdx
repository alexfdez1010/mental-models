This is the capstone. It pulls together everything the course taught: why one model turns every problem into a nail (the man with a hammer), why Munger's cross-disciplinary latticework beats depth in a single field, the three ways models actually combine (they **stack** into a lollapalooza, they **check** each other's blind spots, or they **complete** a picture by explaining different parts), how to run the whole lattice on a live case, how to choose which lenses fit a novel situation, and how a written checklist plus a pre-mortem keep you honest against the four pitfalls. Several questions hide a trap option — watch for the answer that says models merely add up, the one that crowns a single model as enough, the one that treats a stacked reading as PROOF the crowd is right, and the one that shouts "use the lattice!" without naming any specific models. Read every option before you lock.

<Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
The whole course is one argument: no single model is enough, because the man with a hammer bends every problem to fit his one tool and gets no warning at its edge — so build Munger\'s latticework of big models from many disciplines, held at once. Those models combine in three distinct ways: they STACK in the same direction and multiply into a lollapalooza (a powerful, self-reinforcing signal and a danger flag — never proof the crowd is right), they CHECK each other\'s blind spots (a base rate disciplining a vivid story, an incentive auditing a stated reason, second-order effects catching what first-order missed), or they COMPLETE a picture by each explaining a different part. Running the lattice means reading a real case — a bank run, a mania, a red-hot fundraise — through many lenses at once, and the true meta-skill is CHOOSING which lenses fit while your circle of competence tells you when you cannot even choose. Externalise it all with a written checklist and a pre-mortem, and stay guarded against the four pitfalls: forcing, double-counting, confirmation-shopping, and paralysis-by-lattice. Breadth plus discipline plus honesty about your limits — that is the practitioner this capstone was built to make.
</Callout>
