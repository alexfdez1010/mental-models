<Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
"The map is not the territory" means every model is a useful, deliberately
incomplete stand-in for reality — and its value *is* what it leaves out (a 1:1 map
would be useless). Read George Box correctly: all models are wrong, so don't chase a
perfect one or abandon maps altogether; pick one fit for the job and watch its
edges. The gap bites in three predictable places — near the model's **edges**, when
the **stakes** are high, and when the **territory has moved** but the map hasn't —
and they often gang up. The deepest trap is **reification**: mistaking the model,
measure, or category for the real thing, with the tell-tale symptom that doubt
disappears (and its evil twin, Goodhart's law). Stay honest with three habits: hold
maps **loosely**, **check** them against the territory by going to look, and keep
**several** maps of the same ground. And dodge the overcorrection — "trust no maps"
is just paralysis, and a bad map of its own.
</Callout>
