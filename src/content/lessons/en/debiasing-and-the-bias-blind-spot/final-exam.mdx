This is the final exam for **Debiasing & the Bias Blind Spot**. It pulls the whole
course together: why you rate yourself less biased than everyone else (and why that
feeling is worthless as evidence), why merely knowing about a bias barely reduces it,
the two families of real fixes — changing the thinker (outside view, considering the
opposite, pre-mortems, calibration training) and changing the environment (checklists,
red teams, decision hygiene, algorithms, pre-commitment) — the hierarchy that ranks
them, how to match a fix to a bias and to the stakes, and the ways debiasing itself can
mislead you. Several questions look easy until you spot the trap: an "aware" fix
masquerading as a real one, a structural tool aimed at the wrong bias, or over-
correction dressed up as humility. Reason each one through.

<Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
The one idea to carry out of this course: **you cannot think your way out of your own
biases by knowing more about them.** The **bias blind spot** guarantees you\'ll feel
objective while you\'re not, because introspection can\'t see biases that run below
awareness — and that felt objectivity is worthless as evidence. So merely knowing
barely helps. What helps sorts into two families: **change the thinker** (the outside
view, considering the opposite, pre-mortems, and calibration training — real, but they
lean on your willpower) and, more powerfully, **change the environment** (checklists,
red teams, independent estimates before discussion, simple rules, and pre-commitment —
which work whether or not you catch yourself). That gives the hierarchy: **structure
beats incentives beats training beats mere awareness.** Match the fix to the specific
bias and to the stakes, spend heavy hygiene only where a mistake is expensive and
permanent, and stay humble — debiasing shrinks bias, it never removes it. The deepest
move in the whole latticework of judgment is the most humbling one: build the process
precisely because you know you can\'t trust yourself to be objective.
</Callout>
