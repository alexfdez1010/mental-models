<Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
Information is worth exactly what it lets you do differently: the best decision with it minus the best decision without it. That value is never negative, always capped by the stakes, and drops to zero the moment a test cannot change your action — however accurate or cheap it is. EVPI prices a perfect answer and sets the ceiling no real test can beat; it equals the expected cost of the mistake your prior would otherwise commit you to, and it fades as you grow more certain. EVSI prices a noisy test after Bayes deflates its accuracy through the base rate, and you only buy when EVSI clears the price. Gathering information pays off only when genuine uncertainty, a close decision, and high stakes all hold at once — so treat a test as a real option, and watch for the traps: delay, paralysis, accurate answers to the wrong question, base-rate neglect, and information that arrives too late to matter.
</Callout>
