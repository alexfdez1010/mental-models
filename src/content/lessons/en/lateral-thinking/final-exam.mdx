<Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
Lateral thinking isn’t a personality, a licence to be weird, or a rejection of logic — it’s a **disciplined way to reach new starting points**. You question the question to drop hidden assumptions, provoke your brain out of its grooves and **harvest** the movement into something usable, generate wide before you converge, and — the mark of real mastery — you know when NOT to reach for it, letting careful vertical execution handle the problems that already have a method. Run both modes, match them to the moment, and a clever reframe becomes a working answer instead of a clever wrong one. Pass or not, you now have the toolkit. Go step sideways.
</Callout>
