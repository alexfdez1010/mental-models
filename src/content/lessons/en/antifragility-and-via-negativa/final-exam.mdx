This is the whole course in one sitting. Hold the through-line in your head: things meet disorder in exactly three ways — **fragile** things are harmed by it, **robust** things ignore it, and **antifragile** things *gain* from it. The dividing line is not strength but **curvature**: a concave (accelerating-harm) response is fragile, a convex (accelerating-gain) response is antifragile, and by **Jensen's inequality** volatility helps the convex and hurts the concave. From that one idea flows everything — **hormesis** (a small dose of stress triggers a stronger rebuild), the dark twin where **removing** stressors breeds hidden fragility (the turkey, the suppressed fire), the practical fix of **via negativa** (robustify by subtraction — the barbell, skin in the game), the skill of **spotting fragility in advance** (it's a structural property, far more predictable than the shock), and the honest **limits** (the dose matters, survivorship bias, things you just protect). Take a breath. There is no going back once you commit.

<Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
You now hold the whole model. Things meet disorder in exactly three ways — **fragile** (harmed), **robust** (unchanged), **antifragile** (improved) — and the dividing line is **curvature**, not strength: a **concave** response (harm accelerates) is fragile, a **convex** one (gain accelerates) is antifragile, and by **Jensen's inequality** volatility helps the convex and hurts the concave. The biological engine is **hormesis** — a small, survivable dose of a stressor triggers **overcompensation**, rebuilding *more* than was lost (muscle, bone, immunity, desirable difficulties) — with the catch that the dose-response is an **inverted-U**, so only the low-dose zone helps. Its dark twin: **removing** an antifragile system's stressors makes it fragile — the **turkey** mistakes calm for safety, the **Lucretius problem** designs for the worst *observed* shock, **stability breeds instability** (suppressed fires and recessions), and **redundancy/slack** is the hidden strength that over-optimisation strips away. The practical fix is **via negativa**: robustify by **subtraction** — remove the fragilising agent (debt, single points of failure, the toxin) because a known removal beats a speculative addition (**iatrogenics**) — sharpened by the **barbell** (extreme safety plus a little bounded risk, no fragile middle: capped downside, open upside) and **skin in the game** (bear your own downside; it removes the transfer of fragility to others). Best of all, **fragility is far more predictable than the shock** — it's structural and visible now: run the **convexity test** and hunt the tells (debt, concentration, no slack, a calm track record that proves nothing). And the model **lies** at its edges: the **dose** matters (enough stress kills even the antifragile), "what doesn't kill me" is often **survivorship bias**, some fragile things you simply **protect**, "antifragile" is not a synonym for resilience or a licence for recklessness, and you must always **respect ruin**. Never ask only "how do I make this stronger?" Ask: *is this a thing that would gain from a little disorder — and if it's fragile, what can I take away to save it?*
