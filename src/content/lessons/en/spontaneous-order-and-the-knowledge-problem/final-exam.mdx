<Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
No mind — and no database — can hold the knowledge an economy runs on: it lives as
millions of local, perishable, often tacit fragments, and collecting it destroys,
stales, or corrupts it. Prices solve what surveys cannot: each one is a compressed,
money-backed telegram of scarcity that lets strangers economise on tin or cedar
without ever learning why, which is exactly why calculation in kind went blind and
Gosplan optimised against a vanished world. The result is cooperation nobody
designed — pencils, languages, common law, POW-camp cigarette money — grown orders
(cosmos) held together by general end-independent rules, with firms as small designed
islands inside them. But spontaneous never means optimal or just: commons tragedies
and congestion are emergent too, prices reward scarcity rather than virtue, and the
fatal conceit has a lazy mirror in "just let it emerge." The craft is neither
decreeing moves nor worshipping the drift — it is wiring up missing prices and
designing the rules of the game so that what emerges is worth having.
</Callout>
