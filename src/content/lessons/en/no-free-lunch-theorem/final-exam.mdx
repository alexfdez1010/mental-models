<Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
No method beats all the others on every problem — averaged over everything, they
tie, because each method's wins are paid back by its losses. But you never face
"everything": real problems have structure, and there a method whose assumptions
(its prior) match that structure wins big. So the free lunch is a myth, but the
paid lunch — an edge bought with genuine knowledge of the problem — is a feast.
Don't shrug that nothing works (nihilism) and don't crown one favourite (the
universal hammer). Read the structure, match the tool, and know the edge of your
own competence. Carry that, and "what's the best way?" becomes a question you can
finally answer: *best for which problem?*
</Callout>
