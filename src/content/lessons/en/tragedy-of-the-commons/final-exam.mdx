This is the final exam for **Tragedy of the Commons**. It pulls together
everything: the engine (private benefit, socialized cost), what makes a resource a
**common-pool resource** (rivalrous and non-excludable), why each actor is behaving
*rationally* — the **externality** and the **prisoner's-dilemma** structure at its
heart — the limits of the model (when a shared resource is *not* a tragedy), the
real cases (overfishing, antibiotic resistance, traffic, groundwater, the
atmosphere, spam), and the ways out (property rights, regulation and quotas,
**Pigouvian taxes**, and **Elinor Ostrom's** self-governance) along with where each
one fails. Take your time and reason each one through; several look easy until you
spot the trap — blaming character instead of structure, crying "tragedy!" at a
non-rival resource, or reaching for one universal cure.

<Callout variant="success" title="Key takeaways">
The tragedy of the commons is a disaster that needs no villain: when a shared
resource is **free to take but its cost is split among everyone**, each user is
rewarded for overusing it, so individually rational choices add up to collective
ruin. It bites on a **common-pool resource** — **rivalrous** and **non-excludable**
— and it's a **trap, not a vice**: the ruin is structural, running on a **negative
externality** (a cost the taker doesn't pay) and shaped like an **N-player
prisoner's dilemma**. It does *not* bite when the resource is non-rival, excludable
or owned, or governed by a small, watchful group. You'll find the identical machine
in **overfishing, antibiotic resistance, traffic, groundwater, the atmosphere, and
spam** — and an ordinary market price won't save it, because the price leaves the
socialized cost out. The fixes all work by **changing who bears the cost of
taking**: **property rights**, **regulation and quotas**, **Pigouvian taxes** that
price the externality in, and **Elinor Ostrom's** community **self-governance** with
monitoring and graduated sanctions. There is no single cure — *read the specific
commons and match, or blend, the remedy to its structure.*
</Callout>
