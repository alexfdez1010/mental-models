Everybody wants the one best tool — the best diet, the best study method, the
best investing rule, the best algorithm. It feels like it *must* exist; you just
haven't found it yet. The **No Free Lunch theorem** is the surprising, slightly
annoying news that it doesn't. Averaged over *every possible problem*, every
method ties. The clever ones and the lazy ones come out exactly even.

That's not a counsel of despair — it's a redirection. If no method is best
everywhere, then all the performance lives in the **match** between a method and
the particular problem in front of you. "What's the best rule?" is the wrong
question. "Best for *which* problem?" is the right one. This short course builds
that intuition from zero — no proofs, no heavy maths — and hands you a lens you'll
reuse for the rest of your life.
