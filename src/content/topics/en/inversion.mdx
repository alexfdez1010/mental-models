Most thinking runs in one direction. You have a goal — win the deal, finish the
project, stay healthy — and you ask the obvious question: *how do I get there?*
That's **forward thinking**, and it's the right default. But it has a blind spot.
Forward thinking is great at imagining success and terrible at noticing the
handful of ways the whole thing quietly falls apart.

**Inversion** is the move that fixes that. Instead of asking *how do I succeed?*,
you flip the question and ask *how would I guarantee failure?* — list every way to
make absolutely sure the goal dies — and then simply refuse to do those things.
It sounds almost too simple to matter. It is one of the most reliable thinking
tools ever found, championed by the investor Charlie Munger, who borrowed it from
the 19th-century mathematician Carl Jacobi and his three-word rule: *"Invert,
always invert."*

This course assumes only that you've met the idea of a mental model and want a new
angle of attack on hard problems. By the end you'll be able to flip any goal into
its anti-goal, run a **pre-mortem** that surfaces failure before it happens, use
the Munger insight that *avoiding stupidity beats seeking brilliance* — and, just
as importantly, know when inversion is the wrong tool and forward thinking should
lead.
