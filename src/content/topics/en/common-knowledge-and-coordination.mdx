A whole crowd can privately agree on something — the emperor is naked, the
regime is rotten, the bank is shaky — and still not one person moves. Then
someone says it out loud, and in a heartbeat the same crowd acts as one. Nothing
about the facts changed. What changed is that a private truth became a **public**
one: not just *everyone knows*, but *everyone knows that everyone knows.*

That recursive layer has a name — **common knowledge** — and it is one of the
most powerful, least intuitive models in the whole latticework. It is the hidden
switch behind bank runs and revolutions, behind why a Super Bowl ad is worth
more than the same ad shown privately to the same number of people, and behind
why authoritarian regimes fear a public gathering far more than a million private
grumbles. This course builds the idea from the ground up — the ladder from
*private* to *mutual* to *common* knowledge, the coordination games where beliefs
about others' beliefs pick the outcome, the real-world cascades it explains, and
the places where the model is an idealisation that quietly lies to you.
